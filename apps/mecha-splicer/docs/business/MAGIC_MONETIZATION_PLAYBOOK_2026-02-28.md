# Circuit-Mecha Magic Monetization Playbook (2026-02-28)

## Positioning

Circuit-Mecha should not sell itself as "LLM-generated PCB".
Sell as a **verified co-design pipeline**:

- EE + ME constraint-aware generation
- Deterministic gating (DFM + simulation + electrical sanity)
- Automated revision loop
- Client-ready evidence package

This separates you from commodity vibe-coding tools.

## Product Tiers

1. `Draft Pack` (low friction)
- Deliverables: candidate design artifacts + first gate report.
- Use case: idea validation, educational clients.

2. `Verified Prototype Pack` (core SKU)
- Deliverables: full bundle + SIM/DFM risk register + revision notes + lockable BOM.
- Use case: startup prototypes, robotics modules, control boxes.

3. `Execution Support` (high ticket)
- Deliverables: iterative handoff support through manufacturing-prep stage.
- Use case: clients who need engineering guardrails, not just files.

## Where To Monetize (with current economics)

### A) Service marketplaces (fastest cash)

- Upwork
- Official fee model is variable by contract and shown before proposal; standard contracts can range up to 15% according to Upwork pricing docs.
- Use Circuit-Mecha evidence bundle to justify premium fixed-price scopes.

References:
- https://support.upwork.com/hc/en-us/articles/211062538-Freelancer-Service-Fee

## Suggested Priority (Taiwan-friendly, low manual outreach)

1. Upwork service SKU with strict scope and evidence artifacts
2. Apify actor for passive API monetization experiments
3. Whop/Gumroad/Lemon Squeezy for digital packs + subscriptions
4. Tindie only after at least one stable physical design and support workflow

## Pricing Logic

Use value-pricing tied to risk removed, not hours spent.

- Draft Pack: low-ticket
- Verified Prototype Pack: mid-ticket (default offer)
- Execution Support: premium

Raise pricing only after benchmark and delivery pass-rate evidence is attached to proposals.

## Why you still have moat in the LLM era

LLMs can generate drafts quickly. The defensible value is:

- rejection of bad outputs,
- auto-correction under explicit constraints,
- manufacturing and risk evidence,
- repeatable pass/fail benchmark track record.

That is operational engineering leverage, not prompt luck.
