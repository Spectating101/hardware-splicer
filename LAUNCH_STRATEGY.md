## 4. Hardware Ecosystem: Smart Glasses Integration
Circuit-AI is uniquely positioned to leverage the 2025/2026 wave of **Affordable AR Glasses** (RayNeo, Rokid, Viture).

### 4.1 The "Phone-as-Eye" Architecture
*   **Constraint:** Most sub-$300 glasses (Xreal Air 2, Rokid Max) lack integrated cameras for computer vision.
*   **Solution:** Users wear glasses as a **Heads-Up Display (HUD)** while using their smartphone camera as the "Eye."
    *   **Workflow:** User points phone at PCB. Circuit-AI analyzes the feed. Diagnostic overlays are mirrored to the glasses.
*   **Future Proofing:** High-end models (Xreal Ultra, Viture Pro) with native cameras will eventually support direct WebXR camera access, removing the need for the phone.

### 4.2 The Distributed Factory Use-Case
*   **Real-Time Scavenging:** Scavengers use glasses to scan bins of e-waste. High-value chips (e.g., STM32, MOS 6581) glow green in their field of view, enabling instant "Pick and Harvest" decisions.
*   **WebXR Native:** Our Next.js/Three.js architecture is WebXR-ready, requiring zero native app development for most modern AR glasses.
