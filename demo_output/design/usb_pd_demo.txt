### Block Diagram

The proposed circuit architecture consists of the following major blocks:
- **Input**: USB-C PD port
- **Power Management**: Controller for PD protocol and power conversion (e.g., buck converter)
- **Output**: 12V/3A regulated power supply
- **Protection**: Overcurrent protection (OCP), overvoltage protection (OVP), and undervoltage protection (UVP)
- **Indicator**: Optional, for status indication (e.g., power good, fault)

Signal/Power Flow:
- USB-C PD signal lines communicate with the controller.
- The controller manages the power conversion stage based on PD negotiation.
- The output is regulated to 12V and protected by OCP, OVP, and UVP circuitry.

### BOM (Major Parts)

- **USB-C PD Controller**: e.g., STMicroelectronics STM32G0B1, for PD protocol handling and power management
- **Buck Converter**: e.g., Texas Instruments TPS63050, for efficient step-down conversion
- **Power MOSFETs**: e.g., Infineon IRF7413, for high current handling
- **Protection ICs**: e.g., Texas Instruments TPS2594 for OCP and OVP
- **Voltage Regulator**: Integrated within the buck converter or a separate LDO for lower noise applications
- **Inductor**: e.g., Würth Elektronik 744778121, for the buck converter
- **Capacitors**: e.g., TDK C3216X7R1E106M, for input/output filtering and decoupling

### Topology Sketch

- The USB-C PD controller manages communication and controls the buck converter.
- The buck converter steps down the input voltage (up to 20V from USB-C PD) to 12V, using power MOSFETs and an inductor.
- Protection ICs monitor the output for overcurrent, overvoltage, and undervoltage conditions, disconnecting the load or reducing the output voltage as needed.
- Critical components include the controller, buck converter, and protection ICs, which ensure safe and regulated power delivery.

### Safety/EMC Considerations and Test Plan

- **Electrical Safety**: Ensure isolation between input and output, use protective components (e.g., TVS diodes) for transient voltage suppression.
- **Thermal Safety**: Monitor component temperatures, especially the buck converter and MOSFETs, to prevent overheating.
- **EMC**: Use appropriate filtering and shielding to minimize EMI and ensure compliance with relevant standards (e.g., FCC Part 15, EN 55032).
- **Test Plan**: Includes functional testing (output voltage regulation, current limiting), safety testing (overvoltage, overcurrent protection), and EMC testing.
